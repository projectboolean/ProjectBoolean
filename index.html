What is Project Boolean?

Originally launched in fall of 2017 by students at Middleton High School, Project Boolean is a computer science competition for high school students in computational puzzles, problem solving, and programming. This year, we're back for our second iteration.

How does it work?

The event will engage student teams from high schools across Wisconsin in an exciting, fast-paced and fun-filled competition. Over the course of three hours, teams will be tasked with solving a series of computational problems and will will receive points based on how quickly and accurately they solve them. The highest-scoring groups will win prizes, which can be viewed to the left.
