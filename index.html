What is Project Boolean?

Originally launched in fall of 2017 by students at Middleton High School, Project Boolean is a computer science competition for high school students in computational puzzles, problem solving, and programming.

How does it work?

The event will engage around 25 student teams representing high schools across Wisconsin in an exciting, fast-paced and fun-filled competition. In a given period of time teams will have to solve a series of computational problems. Teams will receive points based on how quickly and accurately they solve the challenges, and the highest-scoring teams will win prizes.
